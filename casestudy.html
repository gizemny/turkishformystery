Andrew Harper Auctions

Look & Feel: The former platform was the oldest code in our system. The overall look of the designs was outdated and it wasn’t in-line with the company's new style. My goal was to create an intuitive, easy to navigate and engaging auction design to allow more members to enjoy these fantastic offers which were going unnoticed.

Displays: There was too much wasted space on the landing page, where as the individual package page had too much content. Visually there wasn’t enough emphasis on the important data such as package price and purchase details.

Filter & Sorting: With a large selection of live packages, browsing was difficult so we wanted to add filtering and sorting to the packages.

June 2015 - Kick off: This was a two week design sprint. I started with a content audit and competitive analysis. After the stakeholder meetings, I gained a clear understanding of the requirements, features and elements. I was able to get started on the wireframes, revisions and mockups.

After the mockups were approved, we took a 6 month break from this project.

December 2015: Our front-end developer coded the designs for our team meeting and we re-evaluated the designs. After interacting with the new pages, we decided to make some revisions. Based on feedback from the review meeting, we reworked the "bid bar" to keep all interactions for the bidding in a consolidated area.

After vigorous testing, the project went live on January. The new system requires users to create auction profiles to participate, has max-bid/auto-bid feature, utilizes web sockets for live bidding and option to 'buy now' for our premium members. The new design helps showcase the auction packages and defines a better user experience. Since the redesign, we have seen a significant increase in account creation, bid interaction and sales.
